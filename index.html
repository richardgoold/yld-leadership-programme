02 — The Programme

Seven Themes

Each theme addresses a specific leadership capability, grounded in YLD’s reality. They build on each other, from commercial foundations to operating as one team.

How Each Session Works

Every theme follows the same structure to build understanding, create hands-on experience, and commit to action:

Frame

Context-setting and teaching. Why this matters for YLD right now.

Apply

Hands-on exercise using real YLD scenarios. Groups work, debate, and present.

Reflect

Facilitated debrief. What surprised you? What would you do differently?

Commit

Each person leaves with one specific action to take in the next 2 weeks.

01
Finance 101
How money flows through a consulting business

Outcome

Every leader can read a P&L, understand how their decisions affect margin and cash, and ask the right financial questions with confidence.

Why it matters

Every decision has a financial consequence. Approving a scope extension hits utilisation. Delaying an invoice hits cash. Hiring ahead of revenue is an investment that needs to pay back. Leaders don’t need to be accountants — but they need enough fluency to ask the right questions.

What we’ll cover

  • Revenue mechanics: billable hours, realised rate, revenue recognition, sales-to-delivery lag
  • Cost structure: direct costs vs overheads, gross profit and EBITDA
  • Utilisation & leverage: the two margin drivers — YLD’s 86% vs 76% sector average
  • Cash vs profit: WIP, debtors, DSO — why profit on paper isn’t cash in the bank
  • The quality lens: what a well-run consultancy measures and how your decisions show up in business performance
Workshop Exercise
The YLD P&L Challenge

Teams work through a realistic P&L scenario: a key client pauses, a contractor overruns, and an opportunity to hire ahead of pipeline. You decide — then present the financial consequences.

“Financial curiosity is more valuable than financial jargon. Always ask: how does this affect EBITDA or cash?”

02
Managing Client Relationships
From delivery partner to trusted adviser

Outcome

Every leader sees themselves as a relationship manager and knows how to expand accounts, handle difficult conversations, and generate referrals.

Why it matters

YLD’s growth has been almost entirely organic — word-of-mouth, YLD Champions inside blue-chip clients. Average monthly revenue per customer grew 139%. Top 5 clients stay 30 months. Every delivery lead is a relationship manager whether they realise it or not.

What we’ll cover

  • The relationship spectrum: vendor → delivery partner → trusted adviser
  • Commercial listening: hearing the ask but understanding the context behind it
  • Expanding within accounts: the NewDay playbook — 6 engineers to 31
  • Difficult conversations: scope creep, quality concerns, rate negotiations
  • The referral engine: every project is an audition for the next one
Workshop Exercise
The Client Scenario Room

Three realistic scenarios: an unhappy client, a scope extension without budget, a former contact at a new company. Role-play both sides, then debrief.

“Your best sales team is already in the building. Everyone who delivers for a client is building — or eroding — YLD’s future pipeline.”

03
Leading People
From managing tasks to growing people

Outcome

Leaders shift from “doing the work” to creating the conditions for their teams to thrive — across London, Lisbon, and Porto.

Why it matters

YLD is building a leadership pipeline for a business that intends to scale significantly over the next few years. The gap isn’t capability — it’s understanding what leadership actually is: setting context, making space, creating conditions for others to do their best work.

What we’ll cover

  • The leadership shift: from “I deliver” to “I create conditions for my team to deliver”
  • Delegation vs abdication: ownership with proximity
  • The feedback loop: making feedback normal, not an event
  • Remote leadership: leading across London, Lisbon, and Porto
  • Energy management: sustaining yourself during transformation
Workshop Exercise
The Leadership Mirror

Self-assessment against five dimensions, then anonymous peer input. The gap between self-perception and peer perception is where all growth lives.

“The strongest leaders don’t carry the heaviest load. They build teams that share the load and step in for each other when the work is hard.”

04
Risk, Judgement & Accountability
Owning decisions with incomplete information

Outcome

Risk identification and escalation becomes a normal leadership habit, not something reserved for the CEO.

Why it matters

As YLD grows and the stakes increase, tolerance for unowned risk drops sharply. The team needs to move from “risk is what the CEO worries about” to every leader actively identifying, assessing, and escalating as normal practice.

  • The 70% rule: act when you have 70% of the information, not 100%
  • Escalation without abdication: come with options, not just issues
  • Early warning signs: client, commercial, and people risk
Workshop Exercise
The Pre-Mortem

“It’s January 2028 and YLD has missed its growth targets badly. The business has stalled. What went wrong?” Work backwards from failure to find the risks nobody is saying.

“Asking for support is not a sign of weakness. It is an expected part of working as a strong leadership team.”

05
Trust at Scale
Building and protecting trust as you grow

Outcome

The team can diagnose trust gaps — internal and external — and design specific interventions to strengthen them.

Why it matters

Trust turns a 6-month engagement into 30 months. It creates the Champions who refer business. It’s what gives any stakeholder confidence that the team can deliver — consistently, at scale.

  • The trust equation: credibility + reliability + intimacy ÷ self-orientation
  • Internal trust: between leadership, between offices, with the Board
  • Client trust: built through consistency, lost through silence
  • Psychological safety: where problems surface early
Workshop Exercise
Trust Mapping

Map key trust relationships. Honestly assess each. Identify the one most at risk and design a specific intervention.

“Trust scales through consistency, not heroics. How you behave when things go wrong tells people more than anything you do when things go right.”

06
Commercial Decision-Making
Prioritisation, trade-offs, and sector expansion

Outcome

Leaders apply a consistent prioritisation framework and can take a new sector from blank page to qualified pipeline using AI tools.

Why it matters

The business cannot do everything at once. Being clear about what matters most and sequencing accordingly is part of performing well — not simply working hard.

  • Opportunity cost: every ‘yes’ is a ‘no’ to something else
  • Prioritisation framework: impact, effort, alignment, reversibility
  • Sector expansion playbook: from blank page to qualified pipeline
Workshop Exercise
Sector Expansion Simulation + AI Accelerator

Choose a sector. Use AI tools live to research, draft messaging, stress-test assumptions, and build a prompt library you keep afterwards.

“Prioritisation is not about doing less. It’s about doing fewer things better, and being honest about what you’re choosing not to do.”

07
Running the Business
Not just your function

Outcome

Every leader sees themselves as a leader of the business, not just their function — and can present decisions, not updates, to the Board.

Why it matters

A great business needs a team that runs it as a whole, not people who each run their own area. This means understanding how decisions ripple across functions and holding each other accountable as a team.

  • The systems view: how engineering affects commercial, how sales creates delivery pressure
  • Board-ready thinking: presenting decisions, not updates
  • The rhythm of the business: cadences that create visibility without bureaucracy
Workshop Exercise
The Board Pack Simulation

Prepare a one-page board paper for a real business challenge: problem, options, recommendation, risks. Present to the group acting as the Board.

“You are not just the head of your function. You are a leader of this business. Act accordingly.”

04 — Harnessing AI

AI as a Working Tool, Not a Talking Point

We won’t talk about AI in the abstract. We’ll use it — live, in the session — across three themes that matter to YLD’s growth. Every participant will leave with practical skills and a prompt library they can use immediately.

⚡

Optimise What We Do

Use AI to sharpen sector research, validate market hypotheses, and identify opportunities faster. Turn a week of desk research into 30 minutes of focused prompting.

  • Sector mapping and competitor analysis
  • Identifying digital transformation pain points by vertical
  • Building a qualified target list with entry-point hypotheses
🔧

Be More Efficient

AI for drafting proposals, synthesising feedback, preparing board papers, and creating first-cut messaging. Accelerating the path from thinking to output.

  • Proposal and SOW drafting from brief notes
  • Summarising meeting notes into actions and decisions
  • Building templates for recurring communications
🚀

Evolve the Client Offer

Position YLD as AI-literate. Understand how clients’ needs are shifting, where AI creates new service lines, and how to have credible conversations about AI-enabled transformation.

  • How client buying patterns are changing
  • Where AI creates new service lines for YLD
  • Framing YLD’s AI capability in client conversations

What We’ll Actually Do in the Session

This isn’t a demonstration. Everyone works hands-on with AI tools in real time.

Exercise 1

Sector Expansion Sprint

Teams choose a new sector. Using AI live, they research pain points, draft outreach messaging, identify 5 target companies, and build an entry-point hypothesis — all in 45 minutes.

Exercise 2

The Efficiency Challenge

Each person brings a real task that takes them too long — writing proposals, summarising feedback, preparing updates. We work through it live with AI and build a reusable prompt.

Exercise 3

Client Conversation Role-Play

Practice the conversation: “A client asks how YLD uses AI.” Prepare a credible, specific answer using AI to research the client’s sector and tailor the response in real time.

Output

Personal Prompt Library

Every participant leaves with a curated set of tested, YLD-specific prompts they can use in their daily work from the next day onwards.

Live session prompt — sector expansion

Prompt → I’m a digital consultancy (engineering + design) exploring the UK insurance sector. What are the top 5 digital transformation pain points for mid-market insurers right now? For each, explain what a consultancy like mine would typically be brought in to solve, and what the entry point conversation looks like.


Response → Analysing sector dynamics, regulatory context, and typical buying patterns...

In the session, we’ll run prompts like this live, iterate together, and build a prompt library the team can use immediately. The point isn’t to demonstrate AI — it’s to make it a daily tool.
